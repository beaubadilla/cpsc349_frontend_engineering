Why This Course?

Graduation

Probably the biggest reason why I chose this course was because I needed to take two comsci electives to stay on pace to graduate Fall 2019. However, out of all eligible electives, I did have this course ranked higher than others because I was fortunate enough to gain some experience in Web Dev from the Software Engineering course (CPSC 362) and want to know more.

Career

Web development is definitely a path I can see myself pursuing for my career. But, one thing that deters me from pursuing Web Dev is the need for an artistic eye. I realize that there are web designers/UX designers for that, but I still feel it is a necessary skill.

Professor

Had the professor last semester. Great professor (teaching style, patience to help, difficulty of assignments), which can be hard to find these days.

Experience with Web Dev

I tried to learn HTML/CSS about 2 years ago to make my own personal website, but the block style was too overwhelming for me at the time. I got too frustrated with trying to make it look perfect on every platform/device.

A year later, my group in CPSC 362 decided to make a Web App for our semester project which introduced me to JavaScript, front-end, and back-end concepts.

What am I hoping to learn?

I want to learn how to build a web app from the ground-up by myself. And not only how to build it, but really understand what's going on on the front-end side.
